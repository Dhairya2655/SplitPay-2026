Introduction

The Hidden Tax of Fragmented Finance

Most of us are living a financially fragmented existence — and we barely notice it anymore. You use Splitwise to track who owes whom after a group trip. You open Paytm to pay your electricity bill. You switch to your bank's app to check your balance. Then, when it's time to book a train ticket, you open IRCTC. By the time you've finished what should have been a simple financial task, you've toggled between four apps, re-authenticated twice, and lost context along the way.

This fragmentation isn't just inconvenient — it creates genuine cognitive overhead. Users are forced to mentally reconcile data spread across multiple platforms, and the lack of a unified view makes it genuinely difficult to understand one's financial health. There's no single place to see: what do I owe, what am I owed, what did I spend, and what's left in my account?

Every time a user switches apps, re-enters credentials, and mentally reconciles data from different sources, they're paying a hidden tax. SplitPay proposes that it doesn't have to be this way.

This is the problem that SplitPay was designed to solve — bringing expense splitting, digital payments, banking, and utility services into a single, coherently designed platform.

The Concept

One Platform to Replace Them All

SplitPay began with a simple but ambitious question: What if a single application could replace the entire ecosystem of personal finance tools that most urban Indians rely on? The concept brings together four core domains under one coherent roof.

🔀

Expense Splitting

Group expenses, custom split types, activity tracking, and real-time balance settlement.

💸

Payments

UPI-based transfers, QR scan-and-pay, merchant settlements, and full transaction history.

🏦

Banking

Accounts, passbook, cards, FD tracking, loans, and EMI management — all in one place.

⚡

Utilities & Travel

Bill payments, mobile recharge, flight and train booking, and PNR status tracking.

The value isn't merely additive — it's multiplicative. When expense splitting lives alongside UPI payments, settling a debt becomes a single tap rather than a multi-app journey. When your bank account shares a home with your utility payments, genuine financial clarity becomes possible for the first time.

Core Screens

The Home Screen: Your Financial Command Centre

Fig 2 — Home (3-A) showing Financial Overview, and Payments (5-A) with UPI transfers and bill categories

Upon logging in, users see their total balance and total expense side by side — immediately answering the most important question: where do I stand? A time-filter toggle (Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Year) lets them shift financial perspective without navigating away. The Financial Overview card surfaces revenue and category spend for the selected period, alongside quick-access shortcuts.

This design philosophy — bring the most important information to the surface, let users drill down only when they choose — runs throughout the entire application. The persistent bottom navigation bar (Split · Payment · Utility · Banking) ensures that the mental model of the product is always visible and reachable within a single tap.

Feature Deep Dive

Splitting: Where Fairness Meets Simplicity

Fig 3 — The Split home (4-A): balance summary, friend-by-friend breakdown, and Add Expense CTA

The Split tab delivers an immediate, colour-coded financial picture: green rows for money owed to you, red rows for what you owe, and "settled" markers for resolved balances. The net balance summary at the top eliminates the need for mental arithmetic — users know their position at a glance.

Split Types Supported

SplitPay goes beyond the basic equal split that most apps default to. It supports equal division, custom amounts, percentage-based splits, and ratio-based allocation — because a group dinner where one person ordered three drinks and another ordered water shouldn't be divided evenly, and SplitPay gives users the tools to reflect that nuance precisely.

✦
Payments Module

From Transaction to Confirmation — in Seconds

Fig 4 — Transaction Receipt screens (5-G Confirm Payment) and Mobile Recharge (5-G Recharge)

The payment flow is engineered for confidence. Every transaction receipt surfaces exactly what users need: payee name and UPI ID, payment method, transaction ID, timestamp, and service fee. The "Payment Successful" confirmation uses an animated checkmark and a clean layout to communicate success without ambiguity — no unnecessary text, no confusing states.

The Mobile Recharge screen exemplifies thoughtful personalization: a "My Recharges" section immediately surfaces the user's most frequent numbers, including active plan expiry dates. This contextual memory eliminates repetitive data entry and makes the most common action — recharging for yourself — a two-tap experience.

Onboarding

Creating an Account Shouldn't Feel Like a Chore

Fig 5 — Login (2-A) and Create Account (2-A) screens with social sign-in support

The authentication screens strike a balance between thoroughness and speed. The login screen is deliberately sparse — username, password, social login options — while the sign-up form collects exactly what's needed (name, email, mobile, date of birth, password) without unnecessary fields. Social sign-in via Google, Facebook, and Apple is prominently offered, acknowledging that friction at the gate is one of the most common reasons users abandon a new product.

Design Process

From Sketch to Pixel: The UX Journey

Good UX design isn't a single creative leap — it's a structured, iterative process of understanding, hypothesising, testing, and refining. SplitPay's design journey followed this discipline across four deliberate phases.

01

Information Architecture

Before a single wireframe was drawn, every feature, user journey, and navigation path was mapped. The result: a four-tab bottom nav that provides a clear, always-visible mental model of the application's scope.

02

Low-Fidelity Wireframes (Excalidraw)

Deliberately rough sketches to explore layouts and validate navigation patterns without the distraction of visual polish. Dozens of screens were drawn, discarded, and redrawn — making the important decisions before any investment in aesthetics.

03

Mid-Fidelity Refinement

Typography scales, spacing rhythms, and component hierarchies took shape. Interaction patterns — how the app communicates a successful payment, how the split-type selector guides the user — were mapped in detail.

04

High-Fidelity UI & Prototyping (Figma)

Pixel-precise final designs with a fully linked prototype simulating real navigation — enabling meaningful user testing without a live build.

Primary Teal

Every design decision in SplitPay's visual system was made with intention. Finance is a domain where trust is the most valuable currency, and the design language was crafted to reinforce that trust at every touchpoint.

Why Teal-Green?

A vibrant teal-green primary palette was chosen for its associations with growth, stability, and financial health. Unlike the blue-dominant palettes of traditional banking apps, this green feels modern and approachable without sacrificing seriousness. The light mint-green backgrounds throughout provide visual breathing room and prevent the density of financial information from feeling overwhelming.

Typography for Legibility

Clean, geometric sans-serif typefaces (from the DM Sans and Poppins families) were chosen for their exceptional legibility at small sizes and their neutral, professional character. Financial data — balances, transaction IDs, account numbers — needs to be read accurately at a glance, and the typographic system is engineered to make that effortless.

Research

What Real Users Revealed

Usability testing was conducted via a structured Google Form survey, evaluating the experience across four dimensions: UI clarity, navigation intuitiveness, task completion confidence, and satisfaction with banking and utility features.

✅

Clear Navigation

Users found the bottom tab structure immediately legible — a clear mental model from the first session.

✅

Clean UI

The colour-coded balance system and generous whitespace were consistently cited as standout strengths.

⚠️

Learning Curve

First-time users needed a brief orientation with the multi-module scope — pointing toward a stronger onboarding need.

💡

Key Insight

Users accustomed to single-purpose apps benefit most from a guided first-run experience that reveals features progressively.

Looking Ahead

Future Scope

The roadmap for SplitPay's evolution is rich with possibility. The most compelling direction is toward personalised financial intelligence — using spending patterns and account data to surface insights users wouldn't easily discover themselves. An AI-powered layer could suggest optimal recharge plans, flag unusual spending, or recommend savings strategies based on real behaviour.

Near-term priorities include a guided first-run onboarding experience that introduces modules contextually, advanced spend categorisation with budget tracking, and savings goal visualisation that transforms the app from a transaction tool into a genuine financial planning companion.

Conclusion

One App, Done Well

SplitPay represents a meaningful step toward solving a problem millions of people experience every day without quite naming it: the hidden tax of financial fragmentation. By bringing expense splitting, digital payments, banking, and utility services into a single, coherently designed platform, it delivers something beyond convenience — it delivers financial clarity.

Every design decision documented here — from the information architecture to the teal palette to the split-type flexibility — serves that larger goal. As the product matures and the onboarding experience deepens, there is a real opportunity for SplitPay to become the financial home screen for a generation of users who deserve better than the fragmented alternatives they've been making do with.

The future of personal finance isn't five apps. It's one, done well.
